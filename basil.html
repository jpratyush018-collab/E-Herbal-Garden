🌿 Basil (Tulsi)

The Sacred Healing Herb

Home Plants About Contact

🌱 Basic Information

Common Name: Basil / Tulsi

Botanical Name: Ocimum basilicum (Sweet Basil) / Ocimum sanctum (Tulsi)

Family: Lamiaceae

Origin: India and Tropical Asia

Plant Type: Perennial herb

Parts Used: Leaves, flowers, stems

🌿 Medicinal Uses

✅ Boosts immunity and protects against infections

✅ Helps relieve cough, cold, and respiratory disorders

✅ Reduces stress and promotes mental well-being

✅ Supports digestion and detoxification

✅ Anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties

🌍 Habitat

Basil grows in warm, sunny regions with well-drained soil. It is commonly found in gardens and home pots throughout India and tropical Asia.

🌾 Cultivation

Propagation: Seeds, stem cuttings

Soil: Loamy, well-drained

Sunlight: Full sun

Watering: Moderate; avoid waterlogging

Harvest: Leaves can be harvested throughout the growing season

⚠️ Precautions

High doses may cause mild side effects such as nausea or diarrhea. Pregnant women should consult a doctor before use.

🧪 Chemical Compounds

Eugenol: Anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial

Ursolic Acid: Antioxidant, heart-protective

Rosmarinic Acid: Antiviral, antimicrobial

Essential Oils: Support respiratory and digestive health

💊 Pharmacological Properties

Antimicrobial, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, adaptogenic, digestive aid, respiratory support

🩺 Therapeutic Applications

Used to treat cough, cold, fever, digestive disorders, stress, and anxiety. Boosts immunity and promotes overall wellness.

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

Compounds like eugenol and rosmarinic acid inhibit microbial growth, reduce inflammation, and neutralize free radicals, improving immunity and organ health.

🌍 Modern Relevance

Basil is used in herbal teas, medicinal oils, supplements, and cooking. Tulsi is also a major herb in Ayurveda and modern herbal medicine.

WHO, NCBI, Ministry of AYUSH, Ayurvedic Pharmacopoeia

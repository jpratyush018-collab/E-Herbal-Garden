🧬 Overview

Amla (Phyllanthus emblica) is a powerful medicinal plant known for its high vitamin C content, antioxidant properties, and rejuvenating effects.

🌱 Botanical & General Information

Family: Phyllanthaceae

Common Name: Amla / Indian Gooseberry

Plant Type: Deciduous tree

Habitat: Tropical and subtropical regions

Parts Used: Fruit, leaves, seeds, bark

🌿 Propagation & Cultivation

Seeds: Used for basic propagation

Grafting: Preferred for quality yield

🌞 Growth Requirements

  • Well-drained sandy or loamy soil
  • Full sunlight
  • Drought tolerant
  • Moderate watering

🧪 Key Bioactive Compounds

  • Vitamin C
  • Tannins
  • Gallic acid
  • Ellagic acid
  • Flavonoids
  • Polyphenols

💊 Pharmacological Properties

  • Antioxidant
  • Immunomodulatory
  • Anti-inflammatory
  • Digestive support
  • Metabolic support
  • Liver protection

🩺 Therapeutic Applications

  • Immunity boosting
  • Digestive health
  • Hair & skin care
  • Metabolic health
  • General wellness

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

  • Reduces oxidative stress
  • Boosts immune system
  • Enhances metabolism
  • Protects vital organs

⚠️ Safety & Precautions

  • Safe in moderate amounts
  • Excess may cause acidity

🌍 Modern Relevance

Amla is widely recognized in modern medicine for its antioxidant and immune-supporting properties.

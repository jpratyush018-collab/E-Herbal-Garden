🌱 Basic Information

Common Name: Lemongrass

Botanical Name: Cymbopogon citratus

🌿 Medicinal Uses

Lemongrass is widely used for digestion, stress relief, and antimicrobial benefits. It is commonly used in herbal teas and aromatherapy.

🌍 Habitat

Grows in tropical and subtropical regions with warm climates.

🌾 Cultivation

Primarily grown through division of clumps and requires well-drained soil and sunlight.

⚠️ Precautions

Excess use may cause irritation; essential oils should be used carefully.

🧬 Overview

Lemongrass (Cymbopogon citratus) is a medicinal and aromatic plant known for its citrus fragrance and therapeutic benefits.

Leaves and essential oil are the primary parts used.

🌱 Botanical & General Information

Family: Poaceae

Plant Type: Perennial grass

Habitat: Tropical and subtropical regions

Parts Used: Leaves, stems, essential oil

🌿 Propagation & Cultivation

Division: Most effective method using clumps

Stem Rooting: Can grow roots in water

Seeds: Less common

🌞 Growth Requirements

Warm, humid climate

Well-drained fertile soil

Full sunlight

Regular watering

🌿 Coriander (Dhaniya)

The Aromatic Herb for Health & Flavor

🌱 Basic Information

Common Name: Coriander / Dhaniya

Botanical Name: Coriandrum sativum

Family: Apiaceae

Origin: Mediterranean and Western Asia

Plant Type: Annual herb

Parts Used: Leaves, seeds, roots

🧬 Overview

Coriander is a popular culinary and medicinal herb with aromatic leaves and seeds. It has been used traditionally for digestive, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial purposes.

🌿 Medicinal Uses

✅ Improves digestion and relieves bloating

✅ Antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties

✅ Helps regulate blood sugar levels

✅ Supports heart health and reduces cholesterol

✅ Acts as a natural antimicrobial and detoxifying agent

🌍 Habitat

Coriander grows in temperate to subtropical climates, preferring well-drained, fertile soil with full sunlight. It is commonly cultivated in home gardens and agricultural fields worldwide.

🌾 Cultivation

Propagation: From seeds sown directly into soil

Soil: Fertile, well-drained, rich in organic matter

Sunlight: Full sun for best growth

Watering: Moderate and consistent to keep soil moist

Harvest: Leaves harvested 3–4 weeks after sowing, seeds after flowering

⚠️ Precautions

Excessive consumption may cause mild allergic reactions in sensitive individuals. Use moderately if prone to allergies or digestive sensitivity.

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

Flavonoids and essential oils neutralize free radicals, improve lipid metabolism, and inhibit microbial growth. Leaves and seeds enhance digestion and stimulate metabolism.

🌍 Modern Relevance

Coriander is widely recognized by WHO and NCBI for its medicinal and culinary benefits. It is studied for its antioxidant, antimicrobial, and hypoglycemic properties.

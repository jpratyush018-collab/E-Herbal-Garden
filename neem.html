🧬 Overview

Neem (Azadirachta indica) is a medicinal plant extensively studied for its therapeutic potential and widely used in traditional healthcare systems. It is recognized for its broad spectrum of biological activities, supported by scientific research and traditional knowledge systems.

Different parts of the neem plant — including leaves, bark, seeds, flowers, and oil — are utilized due to their diverse pharmacological properties. Its relevance continues to grow in modern research for applications in healthcare, pharmaceuticals, and natural therapeutics.

🌱 Botanical & General Information

Family: Meliaceae

Common Name: Neem

Plant Type: Evergreen tree

Habitat: Tropical and semi-tropical regions

Parts Used: Leaves, bark, seeds, oil, flowers, roots

Neem is well-adapted to harsh environmental conditions and is known for its resilience and long lifespan.

🌿 Propagation & Cultivation

Seeds (Primary Method): Fresh neem seeds are most commonly used and should be sown soon after collection as viability decreases quickly. Germination usually occurs within 1–3 weeks.

Vegetative Propagation: Methods such as stem cuttings or tissue culture can be used but are less common.

Growth Requirements: Prefers warm climates, full sunlight, and well-drained soil. Requires minimal maintenance once established.

🧪 Key Bioactive Compounds

Azadirachtin, Nimbin, Nimbidin, Nimbolide, Gedunin, Quercetin, Salannin

💊 Pharmacological & Medicinal Properties

Antimicrobial: Effective against bacteria, fungi, and viruses.

Anti-inflammatory: Reduces inflammation and swelling.

Antioxidant: Protects cells from oxidative stress.

Antidiabetic: Supports blood glucose regulation.

Immunomodulatory: Enhances immune response.

Anticancer Potential: Studied for tumor inhibition.

🩺 Therapeutic Applications

Used in skin care, oral health, infections, digestive health, and metabolic disorders.

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

Free radical scavenging, antimicrobial inhibition, inflammation regulation, and immune modulation.

⚠️ Safety & Precautions

Excessive use may cause toxicity. Should be used with proper guidance and is not a substitute for medical treatment.

🌍 Modern Relevance

Recognized by NCBI, WHO, and Ministry of AYUSH as a multi-functional medicinal plant used in integrative medicine and research.

📚 Sources

NCBI, WHO, Ministry of AYUSH (Government of India)

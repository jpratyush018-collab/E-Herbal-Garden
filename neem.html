🧬 Overview

Neem is a highly valued medicinal tree known for its wide range of therapeutic properties. It has been used in traditional medicine for centuries and is now widely researched for modern healthcare applications.

🌱 Botanical & General Information

Family: Meliaceae

Common Name: Neem

Plant Type: Evergreen tree

Habitat: Tropical and semi-tropical regions

Parts Used: Leaves, bark, seeds, oil, flowers, roots

🌿 Propagation & Cultivation

Seeds: Fresh seeds are best; germinate within 1–3 weeks

Vegetative Methods: Stem cuttings or tissue culture

🌞 Growth Requirements

  • Warm climate
  • Full sunlight
  • Well-drained soil
  • Low maintenance after establishment

🧪 Key Bioactive Compounds

  • Azadirachtin
  • Nimbin
  • Nimbidin
  • Nimbolide
  • Gedunin
  • Quercetin
  • Salannin

💊 Pharmacological & Medicinal Properties

  • Antimicrobial
  • Anti-inflammatory
  • Antioxidant
  • Antidiabetic
  • Immunomodulatory
  • Anticancer potential

🩺 Therapeutic Applications

  • Skin care treatments
  • Oral hygiene products
  • Infection control
  • Digestive support
  • Metabolic health

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

  • Free radical scavenging
  • Microbial inhibition
  • Inflammation control
  • Immune modulation

⚠️ Safety & Precautions

  • Use in moderation
  • Avoid excessive consumption
  • Consult professionals for medicinal use

🌍 Modern Relevance

Neem is recognized globally for its role in herbal medicine, agriculture, and pharmaceuticals.

The Queen of Medicinal Herbs

🌱 Basic Information

Common Name: Tulsi / Holy Basil

Botanical Name: Ocimum sanctum (Ocimum tenuiflorum)

Family: Lamiaceae

Origin: Indian Subcontinent

🌿 Medicinal Uses

Tulsi is one of the most important medicinal herbs in Ayurveda. It acts as a natural immunity booster by enhancing the body's defense system. It is widely used in treating cold, cough, asthma, and bronchitis due to its ability to clear respiratory pathways.

Tulsi also works as an adaptogen, helping the body manage stress by reducing cortisol levels. It has strong antimicrobial properties that help fight bacteria, viruses, and fungal infections. Additionally, it supports digestion by reducing acidity, bloating, and improving gut health.

Its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties make it useful in relieving headaches, joint pain, and inflammation.

🌍 Habitat

Tulsi grows naturally in tropical and subtropical regions, especially in India and Southeast Asia. It thrives in warm climates with temperatures ranging between 20°C to 35°C.

🌾 Cultivation

Tulsi is easy to cultivate and can be grown from seeds or stem cuttings.

⚠️ Precautions

Excessive consumption may cause side effects. Consult a doctor if needed.

🧪 Chemical Compounds

Eugenol: Anti-inflammatory
Ursolic Acid: Anti-cancer
Rosmarinic Acid: Antioxidant

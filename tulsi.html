🌱 Basic Information

Common Name: Tulsi / Holy Basil

Botanical Name: Ocimum sanctum (Ocimum tenuiflorum)

Family: Lamiaceae

Origin: Indian Subcontinent

🌿 Medicinal Uses

Tulsi is widely known for its medicinal properties. It helps boost immunity, fights infections, and reduces stress. It is commonly used in treating cold, cough, respiratory disorders, and digestive issues.

🌍 Habitat

Tulsi grows best in tropical and subtropical climates. It requires warm temperatures and thrives in regions with plenty of sunlight.

🌾 Cultivation

Tulsi requires 4–6 hours of sunlight daily. It should be watered moderately and grown in well-drained soil. It can be easily cultivated in pots or gardens.

⚠️ Precautions

Excess consumption of Tulsi may lower blood sugar levels significantly. Pregnant women and people on medication should consult a doctor before regular use.

🧪 Chemical Compounds

Tulsi contains important compounds like Eugenol, Ursolic Acid, Rosmarinic Acid, and Linalool, which contribute to its healing properties.

🧬 Overview

Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) is a well-known medicinal plant widely studied for its adaptogenic and therapeutic properties. It is commonly used to help the body manage stress, improve energy levels, and support overall health.

The roots and leaves of the plant are primarily used in medicinal applications, with the root being the most potent and widely researched part.

🌱 Botanical & General Information

Plant Type: Small shrub

Parts Used: Roots (main), leaves, berries

Ashwagandha is a hardy plant that grows well in dry soils and requires low maintenance.

🌿 Propagation & Cultivation

Seeds (Primary Method):

Seeds are sown directly in soil and germinate within 1–2 weeks.

Vegetative Methods (Rare):

Cuttings are rarely used and less practical.

🌞 Growth Requirements

Prefers dry, well-drained sandy soil

Requires warm climate and moderate sunlight

Needs very little water

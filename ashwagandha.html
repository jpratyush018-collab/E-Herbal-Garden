🧬 Overview

Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) is a well-known medicinal plant widely studied for its adaptogenic and therapeutic properties.

It is commonly used to help the body manage stress, improve energy levels, and support overall health.

🌱 Basic Information

Common Name: Ashwagandha

Botanical Name: Withania somnifera

Family: Solanaceae

Origin: India & North Africa

🌿 Medicinal Uses

Reduces stress, improves stamina, boosts immunity, and supports brain health.

🌍 Habitat

Thrives in dry regions with sandy, well-drained soil.

🌾 Cultivation

Grown from seeds, requires minimal watering and warm climate.

⚠️ Precautions

Should be used in moderation; excessive use may cause stomach upset.

🧪 Key Bioactive Compounds

  • Withanolides
  • Withaferin A
  • Alkaloids
  • Saponins
  • Flavonoids

💊 Pharmacological Properties

  • Adaptogenic
  • Anti-inflammatory
  • Antioxidant
  • Neuroprotective
  • Immunomodulatory

🩺 Therapeutic Applications

  • Stress & anxiety management
  • Cognitive support
  • Immune boosting
  • Energy enhancement
  • General wellness

🌿 Propagation & Cultivation

Seeds: Primary method

🌞 Growth Requirements

  • Well-drained sandy soil
  • Warm climate
  • Moderate sunlight
  • Low water requirement

🦠 Public Health Importance

Widely researched for stress management and neurological health.

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

  • Regulates cortisol levels
  • Reduces inflammation
  • Protects brain cells
  • Supports immune balance

⚠️ Safety

Generally safe when consumed in recommended doses.

🌍 Modern Relevance

Recognized by NCBI, WHO, and Ministry of AYUSH as an important adaptogenic herb.

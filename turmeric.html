🌱 Basic Information

Common Name: Turmeric

Botanical Name: Curcuma longa

🌿 Medicinal Uses

Turmeric is widely used for its anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immune-boosting properties. It supports digestion, healing, and overall health.

🌍 Habitat

Grows in tropical and subtropical regions with warm and humid climates.

🌾 Cultivation

Primarily grown through rhizome propagation in fertile, well-drained soil.

⚠️ Precautions

Excess intake may cause digestive discomfort. Use in moderation.

🧬 Overview

Turmeric (Curcuma longa) is a widely used medicinal plant known for its strong anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and therapeutic properties. It supports immunity and overall health.

The rhizome is the primary part used, rich in bioactive compounds.

🌱 Botanical & General Information

Family: Zingiberaceae

Plant Type: Perennial herb

Habitat: Tropical and subtropical regions

Parts Used: Rhizome

🌿 Propagation & Cultivation

Rhizome Division: Pieces with buds are planted to grow new plants.

Seeds: Rarely used.

🌞 Growth Requirements

Warm, humid climate

Well-drained fertile soil

Partial sunlight

Regular watering

🧪 Key Bioactive Compounds

Curcumin, Demethoxycurcumin, Bisdemethoxycurcumin, Essential oils, Turmerone

💊 Pharmacological Properties

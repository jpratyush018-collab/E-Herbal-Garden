🌱 Basic Information

Common Name: Mint / Pudina

Botanical Name: Mentha

Family: Lamiaceae

Origin: Europe and Asia

🌿 Medicinal Uses

Mint is widely used for its cooling and soothing properties. It helps in digestion and provides relief from indigestion and gas.

It is effective in relieving headaches, nausea, and respiratory problems.

Mint also has antibacterial properties and is commonly used in oral care products.

🌍 Habitat

Mint grows well in moist environments and prefers partial sunlight.

🌾 Cultivation

Mint grows easily from cuttings and spreads quickly in garden soil.

⚠️ Precautions

Excess consumption may cause acidity in some individuals.

🧪 Chemical Compounds

Menthol: Cooling effect
Menthone: Antibacterial
Flavonoids: Antioxidant

🧬 Overview

Mint (Mentha) is a widely used medicinal and aromatic herb known for its refreshing properties and therapeutic benefits. It is extensively used in traditional remedies, culinary applications, and modern healthcare for its digestive, antimicrobial, and soothing effects.

The leaves are the primary part used, containing essential oils that contribute to its characteristic aroma and medicinal value.

🌱 Botanical & General Information

Family: Lamiaceae

Common Name: Mint (e.g., Peppermint, Spearmint)

Plant Type: Perennial herb

Habitat: Temperate and subtropical regions

Parts Used: Leaves (main), stems

Mint is a fast-growing plant that spreads easily and adapts well to different environments.

🌿 Propagation & Cultivation

Stem Cuttings (Primary Method): Healthy stems are cut and placed in water or soil. Roots develop quickly.

Runners (Natural Spread): Mint spreads through underground stems and grows naturally.

Seeds (Less Common): Slower and less reliable method.

🌞 Growth Requirements

Prefers moist, well-drained soil

Grows well in partial to full sunlight

Requires regular watering

Spreads rapidly, so controlled growth is recommended

🧪 Key Bioactive Compounds

Menthol, Menthone, Flavonoids, Rosmarinic acid, Essential oils

💊 Pharmacological & Medicinal Properties

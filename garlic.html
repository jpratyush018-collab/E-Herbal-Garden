🌱 Basic Information

Common Name: Ginger

Botanical Name: Zingiber officinale

🌿 Medicinal Uses

Ginger is widely used for digestion, reducing nausea, and relieving inflammation. It also supports immunity and metabolic health.

🌍 Habitat

Ginger grows in tropical and subtropical regions with warm and humid climates.

🌾 Cultivation

It is mainly grown using rhizome propagation in well-drained soil with partial shade.

⚠️ Precautions

Excess consumption may cause mild irritation. Use in moderation.

🧬 Overview

Ginger (Zingiber officinale) is a widely used medicinal plant known for its therapeutic, culinary, and pharmacological significance. It is extensively studied for digestion, inflammation, and immune support.

The rhizome (underground stem) is the primary part used due to its strong aroma and medicinal value.

🌱 Botanical & General Information

Family: Zingiberaceae

Plant Type: Perennial herb

Habitat: Tropical and subtropical regions

Parts Used: Rhizome

🌿 Propagation & Cultivation

Rhizome Division: Pieces with buds are planted to grow new plants.

Seeds: Rarely used.

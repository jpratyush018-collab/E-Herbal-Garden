🧬 Overview

Giloy (Tinospora cordifolia) is a powerful medicinal plant known for its immune-boosting, anti-inflammatory, and therapeutic properties. It is widely used in Ayurveda for overall health and disease resistance.

🌱 Botanical & General Information

Family: Menispermaceae

Common Name: Giloy / Guduchi

Plant Type: Climbing shrub (vine)

Habitat: Tropical and subtropical regions

Parts Used: Stem, leaves, roots

🌿 Propagation & Cultivation

Stem Cuttings: Most effective method

Seeds: Less commonly used

🌞 Growth Requirements

  • Warm climate
  • Well-drained soil
  • Partial to full sunlight
  • Moderate watering

🧪 Key Bioactive Compounds

  • Alkaloids
  • Glycosides
  • Diterpenoid lactones
  • Steroids
  • Polysaccharides

💊 Pharmacological Properties

  • Immunomodulatory
  • Anti-inflammatory
  • Antimicrobial
  • Antioxidant
  • Antipyretic (fever reducing)
  • Metabolic support

🩺 Therapeutic Applications

  • Immunity boosting
  • Fever management
  • Digestive health
  • Metabolic support
  • General wellness

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

  • Enhances immune response
  • Reduces inflammation
  • Provides antioxidant protection
  • Supports metabolic balance

⚠️ Safety & Precautions

  • Safe in moderate amounts
  • Excess may cause low blood sugar

🌍 Modern Relevance

Giloy is widely recognized for its role in immune support, infection control, and chronic disease management.

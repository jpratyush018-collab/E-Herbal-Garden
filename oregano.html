🌱 Basic Information

Common Name: Oregano

Botanical Name: Origanum vulgare

Family: Lamiaceae

Origin: Mediterranean Region

Plant Type: Perennial herb

Parts Used: Leaves and flowering tops

🌿 Medicinal Uses

✅ Natural antimicrobial and antiviral properties

✅ Relieves cough, cold, and respiratory infections

✅ Supports digestive health and reduces bloating

✅ Anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects

✅ May help in managing minor infections and immunity support

🌍 Habitat

Oregano grows in sunny, well-drained soils. It is commonly found in Mediterranean gardens and can thrive in temperate climates worldwide.

🌾 Cultivation

Propagation: Seeds, cuttings, or division

Soil: Well-drained, sandy or loamy soil

Sunlight: Full sun

Watering: Moderate; do not overwater

Harvest: Leaves harvested before flowering; flowering tops during bloom

⚠️ Precautions

Excess use of oregano oil can cause stomach irritation. Avoid high doses during pregnancy. Use culinary or medicinal forms moderately.

🧪 Chemical Compounds

Carvacrol: Antimicrobial, antibacterial

Thymol: Antiseptic and antifungal

Flavonoids: Antioxidant, immune support

Essential oils: Support respiratory and digestive health

💊 Pharmacological Properties

Antimicrobial, antifungal, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, digestive aid, respiratory support

🩺 Therapeutic Applications

Helps treat cough, bronchitis, cold, digestive upset, and minor infections. Promotes immunity and respiratory health.

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

Carvacrol and thymol disrupt bacterial and fungal cell membranes, reducing infection risk. Flavonoids neutralize free radicals and reduce inflammation.

🌍 Modern Relevance

Oregano is widely used in herbal medicine, essential oils, natural preservatives, and culinary practices for flavor and medicinal benefits.

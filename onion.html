🌱 Basic Information

Common Name: Onion / Pyaz

Botanical Name: Allium cepa

Family: Amaryllidaceae

Origin: Central Asia

Plant Type: Biennial bulbous herb

Parts Used: Bulbs, leaves

🧬 Overview

Onion is a widely used vegetable and medicinal plant known for its flavor and therapeutic properties. It contains antioxidants, sulfur compounds, and flavonoids that promote cardiovascular health and immunity.

🌿 Medicinal Uses

✅ Boosts immunity and helps fight infections

✅ Supports heart health by lowering cholesterol and blood pressure

✅ Anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties help reduce oxidative stress

✅ Helps regulate blood sugar and improves digestion

🌍 Habitat

Onions grow well in temperate regions with well-drained fertile soil, moderate sunlight, and adequate water. They are cultivated worldwide.

🌾 Cultivation

Propagation: From seeds, sets (small bulbs), or bulb divisions.

Soil: Well-drained, rich in organic matter

Sunlight: Full sun

Watering: Regular, avoid waterlogging

Harvest: When tops yellow and bulbs mature (90–120 days from sowing)

⚠️ Precautions

Excess consumption may cause acidity, heartburn, or mild stomach upset.

People with blood clotting disorders should consult a doctor before consuming concentrated onion supplements.

🧪 Bioactive Compounds

Quercetin: Powerful antioxidant, anti-inflammatory

Sulfur Compounds: Cardioprotective, antimicrobial

Flavonoids: Anti-inflammatory, free radical scavenging

Fructooligosaccharides: Prebiotic for gut health

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

Sulfur compounds and quercetin reduce inflammation, oxidative stress, and platelet aggregation while providing antimicrobial effects.

🌍 Modern Relevance

Recognized by WHO, NCBI, and Ministry of AYUSH for medicinal properties. Widely researched for cardiovascular benefits, anti-diabetic effects, and antimicrobial activity.

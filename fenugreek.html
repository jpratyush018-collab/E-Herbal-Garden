🧬 Overview

Fenugreek (Trigonella foenum-graecum) is a widely used medicinal and culinary plant known for its metabolic, digestive, and therapeutic properties.

🌱 Basic Information

Common Name: Fenugreek (Methi)

Botanical Name: Trigonella foenum-graecum

Family: Fabaceae

Plant Type: Annual herb

Habitat: Tropical and subtropical regions

Parts Used: Seeds, leaves

🌿 Medicinal Uses

  • Helps regulate blood sugar levels
  • Improves digestion
  • Supports cholesterol management
  • Provides antioxidant protection
  • Reduces inflammation

🌍 Habitat

Thrives in warm climates with well-drained fertile soil and full sunlight.

🌾 Cultivation

  • Propagation through seeds
  • Well-drained loamy soil
  • Moderate watering
  • Full sunlight

⚠️ Precautions

  • Safe in moderate amounts
  • Excess may cause discomfort
  • Use cautiously in high doses

🧪 Key Bioactive Compounds

  • Saponins
  • Diosgenin
  • Alkaloids
  • Flavonoids
  • Fiber (galactomannan)

💊 Therapeutic Applications

  • Blood sugar management
  • Digestive health
  • Cholesterol control
  • General wellness

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

  • Regulates glucose absorption
  • Improves insulin sensitivity
  • Provides antioxidant effects
  • Enhances digestion

🧬 Nutritional Value

Rich in protein, fiber, iron, magnesium, and vitamins. Supports metabolism and energy levels.

🍽️ Culinary Uses

Widely used in Indian cuisine (methi leaves, seeds in curries, spices, and pickles).

🌱 Propagation Tips

Fast-growing plant. Soak seeds before sowing for better germination.

🌍 Modern Relevance

Used in nutraceuticals, herbal medicine, and dietary supplements for metabolic health.

📚 Sources

NCBI, WHO, Ministry of AYUSH

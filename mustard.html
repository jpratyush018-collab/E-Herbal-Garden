🌿 Mustard (Sarson)

The Golden Oil Seed Plant

Home Plants About

🌱 Basic Information

Common Name: Mustard / Sarson

Botanical Name: Brassica juncea

Family: Brassicaceae

Origin: Asia and Europe

🌿 Medicinal Uses

Mustard seeds are rich in antioxidants and help improve digestion and metabolism.

Mustard oil is widely used for body massage as it improves blood circulation and warms the body.

It is beneficial in relieving muscle pain, joint pain, cold, and respiratory problems.

Mustard also has antibacterial and antifungal properties that help fight infections.

🌍 Habitat

Mustard grows best in cool climates and is commonly cultivated in winter seasons.

It thrives in regions with moderate rainfall and plenty of sunlight.

It is widely grown in India, especially in northern states.

🌾 Cultivation

Mustard is grown from seeds and requires fertile, well-drained soil.

It needs full sunlight and moderate watering for healthy growth.

The crop usually matures within 80–100 days after sowing.

⚠️ Precautions

Excess consumption of mustard may cause stomach irritation or acidity.

Mustard oil should be used in moderate amounts.

People with allergies should consult a doctor before use.

🧪 Chemical Compounds

Omega-3 Fatty Acids: Support heart health
Allyl Isothiocyanate: Anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial
Sinigrin: Detoxifying properties

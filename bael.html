🧬 Overview

Bael (Aegle marmelos) is a medicinal plant celebrated for its digestive, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory properties. The fruit is most commonly used for gastrointestinal health, while leaves, bark, and roots are also valuable in traditional medicine.

🌱 Botanical & General Information

Family: Rutaceae

Common Name: Bael / Bilva / Wood Apple

Plant Type: Medium-sized deciduous tree

Habitat: Tropical and subtropical regions

Parts Used: Fruit, leaves, bark, roots

🌿 Propagation & Cultivation

Seeds: Commonly used method

Grafting: Preferred for better yield and consistency

🌞 Growth Requirements

  • Well-drained soil
  • Warm climate
  • Full sunlight
  • Drought tolerant

🧪 Key Bioactive Compounds

  • Marmelosin
  • Tannins
  • Alkaloids
  • Flavonoids
  • Coumarins

💊 Pharmacological Properties

  • Digestive support
  • Antimicrobial
  • Anti-inflammatory
  • Antioxidant
  • Metabolic support

🩺 Therapeutic Applications

  • Gastrointestinal health
  • Infection control
  • Metabolic health
  • General wellness

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

  • Improves digestion
  • Inhibits microbial growth
  • Reduces inflammation
  • Provides antioxidant protection

⚠️ Safety & Precautions

  • Safe in moderate amounts
  • Excessive intake may cause digestive imbalance
  • Not a substitute for medical treatment for serious conditions

🌍 Modern Relevance & Research

Recognized by NCBI, WHO, and Ministry of AYUSH for digestive and health benefits. Extensively studied for antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and metabolic properties.

🌱 Basic Information

Common Name: Eucalyptus

Botanical Name: Eucalyptus globulus

Eucalyptus is a fast-growing evergreen tree known for its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties, especially in respiratory health.

🌿 Medicinal Uses

🫁 Respiratory Relief: Eucalyptus oil is used for colds, coughs, and asthma.

🦠 Antimicrobial: Contains compounds that help fight bacteria and viruses.

💆 Pain Relief: Used in ointments for muscle and joint pain.

🌿 Aromatherapy: Eucalyptus scent helps reduce stress and mental fatigue.

🩹 Wound Healing: Leaf extracts have anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

🌍 Habitat

Native to Australia, eucalyptus trees grow in temperate and tropical climates. They thrive in well-drained soils and are often found in plantations and natural forests worldwide.

🌾 Cultivation

Sunlight: Full sun

Soil: Well-drained sandy or loamy soil

Watering: Moderate; drought-tolerant once established

Propagation: From seeds or cuttings

Fast-growing tree that can reach large sizes; commonly used for timber, oil, and landscaping.

⚠️ Precautions

Eucalyptus oil should never be ingested in large quantities; may cause nausea, vomiting, or seizures.

Avoid applying undiluted oil directly to skin; may cause irritation.

Keep away from children and pets. Consult a doctor if pregnant or on medication.

🌱 Basic Information

Common Name: Aloe Vera

Botanical Name: Aloe barbadensis miller

Family: Asphodelaceae

Origin: Arabian Peninsula

🌿 Medicinal Uses

Aloe Vera is widely used for its healing properties. The gel inside its leaves is applied to burns, cuts, and wounds to promote faster healing.

It has anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, making it effective in treating acne and skin irritation.

Aloe Vera juice helps in improving digestion, reducing constipation, and detoxifying the body.

🌍 Habitat

Aloe Vera grows best in tropical and semi-tropical regions. It prefers warm temperatures between 20°C to 30°C.

The plant requires well-drained sandy soil and plenty of sunlight. It does not tolerate waterlogged conditions.

🌾 Cultivation

Aloe Vera is grown from small offsets (baby plants) that grow beside the main plant.

It requires very little watering and grows well in pots or garden soil with good drainage.

⚠️ Precautions

Excess consumption of Aloe Vera juice may cause stomach cramps or diarrhea.

Pregnant women should avoid consuming Aloe Vera without medical advice.

🧪 Chemical Compounds

Aloin: Laxative effect
Aloe-emodin: Anti-inflammatory
Saponins: Antimicrobial
Polysaccharides: Healing properties

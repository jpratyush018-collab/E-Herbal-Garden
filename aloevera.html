🧬 Overview

Aloe Vera (Aloe barbadensis Miller) is a medicinal plant known for its soothing, healing, and therapeutic properties.

The gel from its leaves is widely used for skin care and health benefits.

🌱 Basic Information

Common Name: Aloe Vera

Botanical Name: Aloe barbadensis Miller

Family: Asphodelaceae

🌿 Medicinal Uses

Used for skin healing, digestion, immunity, and anti-inflammatory benefits.

🌍 Habitat

Grows in arid and semi-arid regions.

🌾 Cultivation

Grown through offsets (pups) and requires minimal watering.

⚠️ Precautions

Safe externally, internal use should be controlled.

🌱 Botanical & General Information

Plant Type: Succulent perennial

Parts Used: Leaf gel, latex

🌿 Propagation & Cultivation

Offsets (Pups): Primary method

Seeds: Less common

🌞 Growth Requirements

  • Well-drained sandy soil
  • Full sunlight
  • Minimal watering
  • Warm climate

🧪 Key Bioactive Compounds

  • Aloin
  • Acemannan
  • Anthraquinones
  • Vitamins A, C, E
  • Enzymes and polysaccharides

💊 Pharmacological Properties

  • Wound healing
  • Antioxidant
  • Anti-inflammatory
  • Antimicrobial
  • Digestive support

🩺 Therapeutic Applications

  • Skin care
  • Wound healing
  • Digestive health
  • General wellness

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

  • Promotes cell regeneration
  • Reduces inflammation
  • Provides antioxidant protection
  • Fights microbes

🌍 Modern Relevance

Recognized by NCBI, WHO, and Ministry of AYUSH for therapeutic benefits.

🌱 Basic Information

Common Name: Drumstick Tree, Moringa

Botanical Name: Moringa oleifera

🌿 Medicinal Uses

Drumstick (Moringa) is known as a “superfood” because of its rich nutritional and medicinal properties. The leaves, pods, seeds, and flowers are used in traditional medicine.

• Helps boost immunity and overall health.
• Rich in vitamins A, C, and calcium which strengthen bones and improve vision.
• Helps control blood sugar levels and is beneficial for people with diabetes.
• Contains antioxidants that protect the body from harmful free radicals.
• Supports digestion and helps reduce stomach problems.
• Used to improve skin health and hair growth.

🌍 Habitat

The Drumstick tree is native to the foothills of the Himalayas in India but is now widely grown in tropical and subtropical regions around the world. It grows well in warm climates with plenty of sunlight and can tolerate dry conditions. The tree prefers well-drained sandy or loamy soil.

🌾 Cultivation

Drumstick trees are easy to grow and require minimal care.

• Grown from seeds or stem cuttings.
• Requires full sunlight for healthy growth.
• Needs well-drained soil to avoid root rot.
• Moderate watering is sufficient.
• The tree grows quickly and can start producing pods within 6–8 months.

⚠️ Precautions

• Excess consumption may cause digestive discomfort.
• Pregnant women should consult a doctor before consuming moringa in medicinal amounts.
• Always wash leaves and pods properly before use.
• Use in moderate quantities as part of a balanced diet.
